<p>As part of <strong>AI Week</strong>, <strong>Machinery</strong> is organizing a workshop focused on two essential components of modern LLM-based systems: structured outputs and RAG evaluation.</p>
<p>The first session will cover how structured outputs can reduce model errors, enforce schema consistency, and support tasks like document data extraction and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). Tools such as Pydantic and Visual Language Models will be introduced, along with techniques for contextual metadata generation and structured reasoning.</p>
<p>The second session will focus on evaluating RAG pipelines using standard metrics (e.g., recall@k), LLM-based tools like DeepEval and RAGAS, and custom evaluation criteria for citation accuracy, content quality, and formatting. Participants will also define their own metrics suited to real-world applications.</p>
<p>The workshop is intended for professionals developing or evaluating LLM-based systems.</p>
